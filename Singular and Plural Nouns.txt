Singular and Plural Nouns

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, animal, or idea.

Nouns help us identify and talk about the people, objects, animals, places, and ideas around us. Words such as mother, school, book, and happiness are nouns. Understanding singular and plural nouns helps us communicate clearly about one thing or more than one thing.

Singular nouns

Refer to one person, place, thing, animal, or idea.

bottle, cup, pencil, desk, sticker, window, box, watch, wolf, wife, leaf, child, woman, man, mouse, goose, baby, toy, kidney, potato, memo, stereo, sheep, deer, series, species, etc.

Plural nouns

Refer to more than one person, place, thing, animal, or idea.

bottles, cups, pencils, desks, stickers, windows, boxes, watches, wolves, wives, leaves, children, women, men, mice, geese, babies, toys, kidneys, potatoes, memos, stereos, sheep, deer, series, species, etc.
Add -s to Most Nouns

Most singular nouns form the plural by adding s.

bottle → bottles
cup → cups
pencil → pencils
desk → desks
sticker → stickers
window → windows
Add -es to Nouns Ending in ch, x, s, sh, or Similar Sounds

These nouns usually form the plural by adding es.

box → boxes
watch → watches
moss → mosses
bus → buses
Nouns Ending in f or fe

Many nouns ending in f or fe form the plural by changing f to v and adding es.

wolf → wolves
wife → wives
leaf → leaves
life → lives
Irregular Plural Nouns

Some nouns have special plural forms that do not follow regular rules.

child → children
woman → women
man → men
mouse → mice
goose → geese
Nouns Ending in y or o

These nouns may follow different pluralization patterns.

baby → babies
toy → toys
kidney → kidneys
potato → potatoes
memo → memos
stereo → stereos
Nouns with the Same Singular and Plural Form

Some nouns remain the same whether they are singular or plural.

sheep → sheep
deer → deer
series → series
species → species

Using Singular and Plural Nouns in Sentences

I have one child.
I have three children.
A woman is waiting outside.
Five men are working in the garden.
The baby is sleeping.
The babies are playing with toys.
There is a potato in the basket.
There are two potatoes in the basket.
I saw a mouse in the house.
I saw several mice near the field.
